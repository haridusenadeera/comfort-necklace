Hypothesis

The necklace is comfortable and easily adjustable to different users and is able to detect a user's eating episodes

Our Approach

Patients often forget when they consume food and struggle to accurately inform their doctors about their eating behaviors. We intend to create a comfortable necklace that utilizes proximity and audio sensors to collect data and detect the times they consume food.

There have already been some researches on how to detect if someone is eating by using piezo, audio, and electroglottography sensors. And for the piezo sensors, researchers were able to achieve chewing detection accuracy of around 90%. One of the biggest remaining challenges is making the necklace more adjustable and comfortable to wear. Currently, the piezo sensor is tied to the users' neck as a choker and often obese users have trouble putting the necklace on.

Our main goal is to improve the current designs while improving the accuracy of collecting data to detect a user's eating episodes.

Weekly Updates

01/22/2017 Week: 4

We conducted few tests on wireless audio sensor and recognized the pattern of swallowing and chewing. However, there is a lot of background noise. We also read papers about audio data analysis and machine learning algorithms.

01/28/2017 Week: 5

We got a new cabled audio sensor (throat mic) and did tests on it. The recording quality is good and there is only little background noise. We could clearly see the pattern of swallowing and chewing.

02/05/2017 Week: 6

We tested the proximity sensor and successfully received data from it and visualized the patterns. We got an abundance of data in lab environment combining audio and proximity sensors. We are working on the development of Android App for Piezo Sensor. We also intend to conduct tests in the field using audio, proximity and IMU sensors.
